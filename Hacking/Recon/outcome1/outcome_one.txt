Definition of an ethical hacking:

  Ethical hacking refers to the process of exploiting
  an IT system, with the permission of the owner, to
  determine its vulnerabilities and weaknesses.

  The results of ethical hacking are typically
  used to recommend preventive measures that
  mitigate the risk of a cyber attack.

Definition of an ethical hacker:

  An ethical hacker is a person trusted
  to use the methods of ethical hacking
  to penetrate an organisation's
  networks and or computer system.

Unethical hacking process:

  1. Recon.         - Find target; passive and active finger printing.
  2. Scanning.      - Scan the target for vulnerabilities. 
  3. Access.        - Exploit vulnerabilities to gain access to confidential info; rainbow tables.
  4. Exfiltration.  - Modify or keep information.
  5. Sustainment.   - Ensure possible return; root kits
  6. Assult.        - Destroy borked computer (optional).
  7. Obfuscation.   - Cover tracks.

Pentesting process:

  The process is concerned with 1, 2, 3, 5 and 7 of the
  above process.

Box testing:

  Black boxes are unknown, white are known completely and grey are known to a minimal degree.
